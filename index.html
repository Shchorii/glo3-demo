A note from the founders

We spent a decade in adtech watching a strange thing happen: the local bakery on the corner could buy a billboard in Times Square more easily than it could buy an ad on the local news site three doors down.

The reason isn't that hyperlocal advertising is hard. The reason is that the systems built for national brands don't unbundle to the block. Glo is the unbundling.

Brooklyn first. Tel Aviv and Austin next. If you operate a small business that lives on foot traffic — or a publisher whose audience is the neighborhood — we'd like to talk.

FAQ

Common questions.

How does block-level targeting actually work?

Glo enriches each ad request with a confidence-scored block-level location derived from publisher-side signals — panel data, IP, declared geo, intent. We don't depend on third-party cookies or device IDs. Targeting is evaluated at bid-time against the campaign's radius, not pre-segmented audiences.

Which publishers are in the inventory?

47 local-news, neighborhood-blog, weather, transit, and lifestyle publishers operating in each launch city. The full list is shared with verified advertisers in onboarding. New inventory is added monthly through a joint venture with a premium publisher network.

How is this different from Google Local Service Ads or Meta's local targeting?

Two structural differences. First, granularity: Glo runs at the block, not the zip code or one-mile radius. Second, supply: Glo owns dedicated publisher inventory rather than bidding into open exchanges, which is what lets us hold the price flat at $29/day instead of running auction CPMs against national brands.

Can I run ads in multiple neighborhoods or cities?

Yes. Each block is a separate $29/day line item. Multi-block discounts kick in at five blocks. Multi-city is supported once a launch city is live — Brooklyn Q3 2026, Tel Aviv Q4, Austin Q1 2027.

Do you make the ad creative, or do I bring my own?

Bring your own image and a one-line offer. We provide free creative help during the early-access program — first 500 advertisers get a one-time design pass.

What about cities outside the launch list?

Join the waitlist and tell us which neighborhood. The fourth city is being chosen partly on operator demand.
